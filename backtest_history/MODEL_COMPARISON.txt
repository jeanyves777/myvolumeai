                              KEY OBSERVATIONS
====================================================================================================

1. SIGNIFICANTLY HIGHER RETURNS
   The advanced model produced nearly 3x the returns ($13,974 vs $4,819).
   This is likely due to more realistic options pricing capturing larger
   intraday price swings.

2. BETTER RISK-ADJUSTED PERFORMANCE
   - Profit Factor improved from 4.84 to 10.37 (2x better)
   - Max Drawdown reduced from 3.58% to 1.92% (46% improvement)
   - Calmar Ratio improved from 1.35 to 7.27 (5x better)

3. OPTIONS PRICING DIFFERENCES
   The advanced model uses:
   - IV Smile/Skew: OTM puts have ~15% higher IV than ATM
   - Intraday IV Patterns: +8% IV at open/close, -0% midday
   - 0DTE Premium: +15% IV boost for same-day expiry options
   - These features make cheap options (like $0.32 calls) more volatile

4. NOTABLE TRADES (Advanced Model)
   - Trade #6 (Nov 8): +169.57% on $0.32 calls -> $2,933.17 profit
   - Trade #12 (Nov 15): +289.18% on $0.35 puts -> $4,964.44 profit
   These massive wins came from cheap near-expiry options that saw
   huge IV expansion under the advanced model.

5. WHY RESULTS DIFFER
   The simple model used flat 80% IV for all options. The advanced model
   uses dynamic IV (85% base for COIN with smile/skew adjustments), which:
   - Makes cheap OTM options more expensive (higher IV = higher premiums)
   - Creates more volatility in options near expiry
   - More accurately simulates real market conditions

                              CONCLUSION
====================================================================================================

The advanced options pricing model produces significantly better backtest results,
but these results should be interpreted cautiously:

PROS:
- More realistic representation of options behavior
- Captures IV dynamics that occur in real markets
- Better models the premium for 0DTE options

CONS:
- Results may be OVERLY optimistic because:
  - Real bid-ask spreads can be wider
  - Real slippage can be higher
  - Model assumes you can always fill at mid
  - Synthetic options don't have real liquidity constraints

RECOMMENDATION:
Use the advanced model for backtesting, but apply a conservative discount
(20-30%) to expected returns when planning live trading.
